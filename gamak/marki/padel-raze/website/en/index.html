Engineering for European Climate

Built to withstand
any European winter.

SPHC Steel 100×100×3mm

Main columns 100×100×3mm, load-bearing beams 100×50×3mm. The 3mm wall thickness eliminates vibrations in strong winds and protects glass from cracking — a standard required in European wind zones.

Hot-Dip Galvanizing After Welding — 3 Layers

The finished welded structure is immersed in zinc at 450°C — only after welding. Zinc penetrates joints and profile interiors. Then powder coating is applied. Result: C5 corrosion class, 25+ years without corrosion in European climate.

UDEM Certificates × 4 + TÜV Rheinland

4 UDEM certificates for: steel (EN 10219), glass (EN 12150‑1+2), surface (EN 14877) and LED (EN 60598). Glass certified by TÜV Rheinland. Documentation ready for public procurement — no rejection on technical grounds.

LED Lighting 8×200W · 300 Lux · 6500K

8 symmetrically placed lights — zero shadows during smashes. 6500K temperature ideal for video streaming. In the Full Panoramic model, lighting masts are integrated into column structure.

Court Lines Woven Into Turf

White lines are embossed during turf production — not glued on site. Result: geometrically perfect lines, durable throughout the entire surface lifespan.

Glass Connectors 5mm · 304 Stainless Steel

18 panels of 12mm glass are held by 304 stainless steel connectors with 5mm thickness. 304 steel does not rust for decades outdoors — glass is secure for the entire court lifespan.

Machine-Welded Mesh 1:1 Automatically

Every wire intersection machine-welded — not by hand, not selectively. Smooth, polished surface with no sharp edges. 4mm wire, 50×50mm mesh, hot-dip galvanized Q235B.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

How much does a turnkey padel court cost?
The price depends on the model and location. PADEL RAZE offers 3 models — RAZE CLASSIC, RAZE PRO and RAZE PANORAMA. The price includes hot-dip galvanized C5 steel structure, 12mm TÜV Rheinland tempered glass, turf surface, LED lighting and installation. Contact us for an individual quote.
Can municipalities get co-financing for padel courts?
Yes. Municipalities can apply for EU and national sports program co-financing up to 80% of investment costs. PADEL RAZE assists with funding application preparation — free of charge. Procurement documentation with UDEM and TÜV certificates is ready to submit.
How long does it take to build a padel court?
Complete delivery takes 10–16 weeks: building design (2–4 wks), production and transport (6–10 wks), installation and handover (1–2 wks). When ordered by March, the court is ready for the summer season.
What certificates do PADEL RAZE courts have?
4 UDEM certificates: steel (EN 10219), glass (EN 12150‑1+2), surface (EN 14877), LED lighting (EN 60598). Glass certified by TÜV Rheinland. C5 corrosion class structure (hot-dip galvanized after welding). Documentation ready for public procurement.
What is the return on investment for a padel court?
With rental prices of €20–30/hour and average occupancy of 5–8 hours per day, one outdoor court generates €3,000–7,000 per month. Typical ROI: 2–3 years. Leasing financing available.
