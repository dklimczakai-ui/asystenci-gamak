About us

15 years of building ice rinks and sports infrastructure

We specialise in serving local governments and municipalities. We understand public procurement procedures, know how to prepare documentation and meet deadlines resulting from grants. Funding

MSiT Programme 2026 — PLN 400 million for sports infrastructure

The Polish Ministry of Sport and Tourism has launched the Regional Sports Infrastructure Development Programme. Budget: PLN 200 million from FRKF funds + co-financing from regional authorities = PLN 400 million in total. Applications open until 15 March 2026.

Funding covers up to 60% of eligible expenditure. Applications are submitted by local governments, clubs and sports associations through the AMODIT system.

GAMAK helps prepare your application and technical documentation — free of charge.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What does GAMAK do?

GAMAK builds turnkey sports infrastructure — seasonal and hockey ice rinks, field-rink 2in1 facilities, sport surfaces, padel courts and supplies ice resurfacers. We serve municipalities, sports clubs and private investors across Poland.

How much does it cost to build an ice rink?

The price depends on the type of rink (seasonal, hockey, 2in1), surface area and equipment. Seasonal ice rinks start from approx. PLN 300,000. We prepare a detailed quote after a consultation — free of charge.

Does GAMAK help with funding applications?

Yes. We provide free support to municipalities in preparing technical documentation for applications under the MSiT programmes, Polish Deal and European funds. We have experience with many successful applications.

Where does GAMAK operate?

Across Poland. Our headquarters are in Jastrzebie-Zdroj (Silesia region), but we carry out projects throughout the country — from Pomerania to the Subcarpathian region.

What certifications do GAMAK ice rinks have?

Hockey rinks hold IIHF (International Ice Hockey Federation) and PZHL (Polish Ice Hockey Association) certification. Boards and structures comply with PN-EN and CE standards.

How long does it take to build an ice rink?

A seasonal (mobile) ice rink is assembled in a few days to two weeks. A hockey rink (permanent, with piping embedded in concrete) takes several months up to over a year. A 2in1 facility — the timeline is determined individually.
