What is a 2-in-1 facility

A field in summer. An ice rink in winter. One facility — all year round.

A field-rink 2-in-1 is a sports facility that combines two functions in a single structure. In summer, it operates as a multisport field — for basketball, volleyball, football, tennis, roller skating, and inline hockey. In winter, the same surface serves as a base for an ice sheet for ice skating, ice hockey, and curling.

The refrigeration system is permanently installed beneath the surface — no seasonal assembly and disassembly required. GAMAK has completed over 15 two-in-one facilities in Poland, including in Czestochowa, Wroclaw, Malbork, Debica, and Muszyna.

How it works

How does a field-rink 2-in-1 work?

The facility consists of three layers: an aggregate sub-base, a permanently installed refrigeration system (glycol pipelines), and a top sports surface that is permeable to water. In winter, the chiller unit initiates freezing — the ice sheet forms directly on the surface. After the winter season, the ice melts, meltwater passes through the surface, and the field is immediately ready for summer use.

The difference from mobile ice rinks is fundamental: there is no need to assemble and disassemble the refrigeration system every season, eliminating logistics costs and the risk of equipment failures. Only the dasher boards need to be stored.

Surface variants

Two surface variants

GAMAK offers the field-rink 2-in-1 in two surface variants — depending on planned disciplines and usage conditions.

Premium variant

EPDM "UNI ICE" Surface

Surface made from EPDM granules bonded with flexible polyurethane resin. The refrigeration system is installed beneath the EPDM layer on a stone aggregate sub-base.

  • Permeable to meltwater — no removal required for freezing
  • UV-resistant — does not fade or degrade
  • Flexible and comfortable — cushions falls
  • Layered installation — on existing sub-base
  • Solar heat recovery capability

Summer disciplines: volleyball, basketball, tennis, handball, football, badminton

Inline hockey & skating

Polypropylene Surface (Stilmat Outdoor Inline)

Modular polypropylene surface laid on a permeable layer with an installed refrigeration system. Designed for roller skating and inline hockey — low friction coefficient.

  • Resistant to mechanical damage — intensive use
  • Maintenance-free — standard cleaning only
  • Stable in temperature and humidity variations
  • Can remain under ice throughout winter without removal

Summer disciplines: inline hockey, roller skating, basketball, volleyball, tennis, football, badminton

2-in-1 advantages

Advantages of a field-rink 2-in-1 over a mobile ice rink

2-in-1 facilities solve three problems that mobile ice rink operators face.

No seasonal disassembly

The refrigeration system is permanently installed beneath the surface. No need to disassemble in spring and reassemble in autumn — saving weeks of work and logistics costs every year.

Instant readiness after winter

After the ice sheet melts, the surface is immediately ready for sports use. No downtime between seasons.

Storage space savings

Only the dasher boards need to be stored. The entire refrigeration system, surface, and sub-base remain in place year-round.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a field-rink 2-in-1 differ from a mobile ice rink?

A mobile ice rink requires assembling and disassembling the entire refrigeration system every season. In a 2-in-1 facility, the system is permanently installed beneath the sports surface. There is no need to disassemble it — simply start the chiller in autumn and shut it down in spring.

Which surface to choose — EPDM or polypropylene?

EPDM (UNI ICE) is flexible, cushions falls, and works well for recreational facilities. Polypropylene (Stilmat Outdoor Inline) is harder, more mechanically resistant, and better suited for inline hockey and roller skating. The choice depends on the planned disciplines.

Can the surface withstand seasonal freezing and thawing?

Yes. Both surfaces — EPDM and polypropylene — are designed for multi-year freezing and thawing cycles without losing their properties. The polypropylene surface can remain under ice throughout the entire winter season without removal.
