What we do

Professional Hockey Rinks — From Design to Certification

A hockey rink is a permanent sports facility designed for professional ice hockey competitions — with cooling piping embedded in a concrete slab, 60x30 m dasher boards and full equipment compliant with IIHF (International Ice Hockey Federation) and PZHL (Polish Ice Hockey Association) requirements.

GAMAK delivers hockey rinks turnkey — from design and cooling system construction, through dasher board and glazing installation, to player boxes, goals and safety nets.

Cooling System

Cooling System — In-Slab Piping in Concrete

A professional hockey rink requires a permanent cooling system embedded directly in the concrete slab. Cooling pipes laid in the concrete ensure even temperature distribution across the entire ice surface — from board to board, with no warmer or colder zones.

In-Slab Piping

Durable, no seasonal installation required. The system is permanently built into the facility structure.

Year-Round Operation

The rink operates regardless of weather conditions. Consistent ice surface maintained throughout the entire season.

Even Temperature Distribution

Stable ice quality across the entire 60x30 m surface. No warmer or colder zones.

Low Operating Costs

Efficient cooling circuit reduces energy consumption. Optimal chiller unit performance.

Hockey Dasher Boards

IIHF Hockey Dasher Boards — Construction and Specifications

Hockey dasher boards enclose the ice surface and must meet federation requirements for the facility to obtain certification for league and international competitions.

Parameter Value
Ice Surface Dimensions 60 x 30 m (IIHF standard)
Board Perimeter 166 linear meters
Construction Hot-dip galvanized steel + HDPE panels
Certification IIHF / PZHL
Glazing Tempered glass or acrylic

The hot-dip galvanized steel structure is corrosion resistant and withstands years of use in ice rink conditions (humidity, low temperatures, puck impacts).

HDPE (high-density polyethylene) panels serve as infill — they are durable, safe on player impact and easy to maintain.

Equipment

Additional Hockey Rink Equipment

A complete hockey rink is more than just an ice surface and boards. IIHF and PZHL regulations require additional equipment for competitive play.

Player Boxes

Team benches with protective shielding, compliant with federation regulations. Separate penalty boxes with independent ice access.

Glazing

Tempered glass or acrylic panels installed behind the goals and along straight sections of the boards. Protects spectators while providing full visibility.

Hockey Goals

Certified goals of regulation dimensions. Compliant with IIHF and PZHL requirements.

Safety Nets

Installed above the boards and glazing to protect spectators from flying pucks.

Each piece of equipment is individually selected based on the facility specification and level of competition (national league, international competitions, training).

Certificates and Standards

Certificates and Standards

GAMAK hockey rinks meet the requirements of four standards.

IIHF
International Ice Hockey Federation. Certification covers ice surface dimensions, dasher board construction and equipment. Required for hosting international matches.
PZHL
Polish Ice Hockey Association. Compliance with national requirements for league competitions.
PN-EN
Polish Standards harmonized with European standards. Covering the safety of steel structures, materials and glazing.
CE
Conformity marking with European Union directives regarding safety of use.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a hockey rink differ from a recreational ice rink?

A hockey rink measures 60x30 m (IIHF standard), features a cooling system embedded in a concrete slab (permanent, year-round) and certified dasher boards for competitive play. A recreational rink is typically smaller, mobile (seasonal) and does not require federation certification.

Can league games be held on a GAMAK hockey rink?

Yes. Rinks with PZHL certification meet the requirements for Polish Hockey League and lower league competitions. With IIHF certification, the facility also qualifies for international matches.

How much does a hockey rink cost?

The price depends on the specification — cooling system, type of dasher boards, additional equipment and scope of construction works. We prepare individual quotes after consultation. A free needs analysis is the first step.

Does GAMAK assist with funding applications?

Yes. We support municipalities in preparing technical documentation for grant applications from MSiT programmes, Polish Deal and EU funds.

What are hockey dasher boards made of?

The load-bearing structure is hot-dip galvanized steel (corrosion resistant). The infill panels are made of HDPE (high-density polyethylene) — durable, safe on player impact and easy to replace. Glazing consists of tempered glass or acrylic.

How long does it take to build a hockey rink?

The timeline depends on the scope of works and site conditions. A typical project — from construction start to commissioning — takes from several months to over a year. A detailed schedule is established during the design phase.
