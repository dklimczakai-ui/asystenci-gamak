About the Product

Polypropylene Inline Hockey Surface — What It Is and How It Works

The inline hockey surface is a modular polypropylene floor installed on ice rink arenas outside the winter season. Each module connects to the next using a snap-lock system — no gluing, drilling or specialised tools needed.

The surface is designed for optimal slide coefficient for inline skate wheels and proper puck handling. This surface was used at the Inline Hockey World Championships.

The surface holds a PZH certificate (National Institute of Hygiene), fire resistance certification and meets EU standards. Over 125 installations in Poland and Europe.

Variants

Two Surface Systems — Seasonal and Permanent

We match the variant to the facility's needs. Both systems proven in over 125 installations in Poland and Europe.

Stilmat R-Evo — Seasonal System

Installed after defrosting the ice rink, removed before the winter season. Triangular surface pattern providing optimal slide for inline skates and puck handling.

  • Inline hockey, futsal, basketball, badminton
  • Collaboration with world-class hockey clubs
  • Used at FIRS World Championships (inline hockey)
  • First installation: 2-3 days, subsequent seasons: 1 day

Stilmat Outdoor Inline — Permanent System

The surface stays on the arena year-round — no disassembly needed. Water-permeable, allowing ice to be frozen on top. The surface remains under the ice.

  • Designed for outdoor use (open-air rinks)
  • Low friction coefficient
  • Stability across temperature fluctuations
  • Eliminates annual installation and removal

How It Works

Your Ice Rink Operates 12 Months a Year

A standard ice rink generates revenue for 4-5 months a year. The rest of the time, the arena sits empty.

The inline hockey surface changes that. After the winter season ends, the modules are installed directly on the rink floor. The facility starts operating as a sports arena — for inline hockey, floorball, box lacrosse, roller derby, artistic roller skating, basketball, volleyball or tennis.

Before the next winter season, the surface is removed. The ice rink returns to normal operation. No permanent modifications to the facility.

Installation process

  1. End of the ice rink season and drying of the arena
  2. Laying out the surface modules (snap-lock system — no specialised tools)
  3. Applying sports discipline lines in different colours
  4. Installing boards (if required by the discipline)
  5. Arena ready for use

Installation time: first installation takes 2-3 days, subsequent seasons — just 1 day. Disassembly follows the reverse process. Modules are stored in compact stacks — requiring minimal space.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an inline hockey surface?

It is a removable polypropylene surface installed on ice rink arenas outside the winter season. It enables year-round use of the facility for sports such as inline hockey, floorball, box lacrosse, roller derby, artistic roller skating, as well as basketball, volleyball, tennis and handball.

Does the surface have certifications?

Yes. The surface holds a PZH certificate (National Institute of Hygiene), fire resistance certification and meets EU standards. This surface has been used at the Inline Hockey World Championships (FIRS). Over 125 projects completed in Poland and Europe.

What sports can be played on this surface?

The surface supports: inline hockey, floorball, box lacrosse, roller derby, artistic roller skating, basketball, volleyball, tennis, handball and badminton. Lines for individual sports are applied in different colours.

Can the surface be installed on an existing ice rink?

Yes. The surface is designed for installation on ice rink arenas — both sports and recreational. Installation is quick and requires no permanent modifications to the facility. After the summer season, the surface is removed and the ice rink returns to normal operation.

What sizes is the surface available in?

The surface is available in various configurations — from compact 20x40 m arenas, through 26x60 m, to full-size 30x60 m courts. The size is customised to the facility dimensions.

Does GAMAK help with funding applications?

Yes. We support municipalities in preparing technical documentation for grant applications from MSiT programmes, the Polish Deal and European funds — free of charge.

How much does an inline hockey surface cost?

The price depends on arena size and configuration. We prepare an individual quote after consultation — free of charge. Contact us by phone (+48 534 550 590) or through the form.

How long does surface installation take?

First installation on an ice rink arena takes 2-3 days. Subsequent seasons — just 1 day. The modular snap-lock system requires no specialised tools or construction crews.

Can the surface be used on an outdoor (open-air) ice rink?

Yes. The surface is resistant to UV, frost, rain and temperature fluctuations. It performs well in both ice halls and outdoor rinks. Water does not pool on the surface — play is possible immediately after rain.

How does an inline hockey surface differ from a regular sports surface?

The inline hockey surface has an optimal slide coefficient designed for inline skate wheels and puck handling. Standard sports surfaces (e.g. for basketball) do not provide these parameters. This surface was used at the Inline Hockey World Championships.

What are the surface variants — seasonal and permanent?

Two variants are available. Seasonal system (Stilmat R-Evo) — installed after defrosting the ice, removed before winter. Permanent system (Stilmat Outdoor Inline) — water-permeable surface that stays on the arena year-round, no disassembly needed for freezing. The surface remains under the ice.

What certifications does the surface hold?

The surface holds a PZH certificate (National Institute of Hygiene), fire resistance certification and meets EU standards. Used at the Inline Hockey World Championships (FIRS). Over 125 projects completed in Poland and Europe.
