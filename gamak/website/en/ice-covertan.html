ICE COVERTAN systems®

ICE COVERTAN — Modular Isothermal System

ICE COVERTAN systems® is a modular isothermal floor that allows you to cover the ice sheet and convert an ice rink into a multi-purpose arena — without defrosting. Installation takes 4-6 hours with a team of 4-6 people. After removing the floor, the ice rink is ready for use again without re-freezing.

The system enables organising concerts, trade fairs, exhibitions, galas and sports competitions at ice rink facilities, increasing the number of operational days and venue revenue.

How It Works

How Does the Isothermal Floor Work?

The ICE COVERTAN floor consists of modules laid directly on the ice sheet using puzzle-type interlocking connections. The modules create a sealed, thermally insulated surface that isolates the ice from the arena's heat.

Puzzle-type modules

Modules laid directly on the ice sheet, connected by perimeter locks — no specialised tools required.

Insulation 0.044 W/mK

The ice under the floor does not melt — thermal insulation effectively isolates the sheet from the arena's heat.

30-40% energy savings

No defrost-refreeze cycle. After removing the floor, the ice rink is immediately ready for use.

After the event, the modules are removed in 3-5 hours and the ice rink is immediately ready for use. There is no need to defrost the ice before the event or refreeze it afterwards — saving 30-40% of energy compared to the traditional defrost-refreeze cycle.

Construction

Module Construction — Two Layers

Each ICE COVERTAN module consists of two layers of thermoplastic materials, providing mechanical strength and thermal insulation.

Top plate (wear layer)

  • High-impact thermoplastic material — resistant to abrasion, impacts and mechanical damage
  • Anti-slip surface — friction coefficient μ ≥ 0.4
  • Chemical resistance — to cleaning agents used at events
  • Integrated screw fixings
  • Standard colour: black. Other colours available on request

Bottom plate (insulation layer)

  • Three-part construction increasing thermal insulation
  • Microcellular structure — thermal insulation properties at low weight
  • Puzzle-type perimeter locks — connection without specialised tools
  • Air chambers — additional thermal barrier between ice and surface
  • Ventilation channels — prevent moisture condensation
  • Reinforcements in areas of increased load

GAMAK Ice Cover

GAMAK Ice Cover Conversion Panels — Cover the Ice Rink Without Defrosting

GAMAK Ice Cover is a conversion panel system with a waffle/grid structure, designed to cover the ice sheet and convert an ice rink into a multi-purpose arena. The panels are made from patented materials combining mechanical strength with thermal insulation.

The system is used in multi-purpose arenas for organising basketball games, concerts, trade fairs and commercial events at ice rink facilities.

GAMAK Ice Cover Construction

How Are GAMAK Ice Cover Panels Built?

The panels use a patented waffle/grid structure that provides rigidity, load-bearing capacity and thermal insulation in a single composite layer.

Waffle/grid structure

The internal rib network distributes loads evenly across the entire panel surface. The construction provides rigidity at low self-weight.

Patented insulation materials

The thermoplastic composite protects the ice sheet from the arena's heat. The ice remains frozen throughout the entire event without defrosting.

Durability and maintenance-free

Panels require no maintenance. Material resistant to abrasion and repeated installation/disassembly. Proven in environments with frequent surface conversions.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ice under the ICE COVERTAN floor melt?

No. The floor's thermal conductivity is 0.044 W/mK — the thermal insulation effectively isolates the ice sheet from the arena's heat. The ice remains frozen throughout the entire event.

How long does it take to install the isothermal floor?

Installation on a standard ice rink (1,800 m²) takes 4-6 hours with a team of 4-6 people. Disassembly is faster — 3-5 hours. No specialised equipment is required.

How much does the ICE COVERTAN system weigh?

6.2 kg per square metre. For a standard 1,800 m² ice rink, that is 11,250 kg in total. The low weight minimises the load on the ice sheet.

What load can the floor withstand?

Up to 0.25 MPa, i.e. 1,019 kg per 200x200 mm foot. This is sufficient for setting up concert stages, grandstands, trade fair booths and heavy event equipment.

How much storage space does the system require?

Approximately 37 m² (optimised down to 30 m²). Modules are stored on pallets measuring 1,850 x 1,280 mm, stacked up to 2.2 m high (55 modules per pallet).

How long does the isothermal floor last?

The manufacturer's warranty is 5 years. Expected lifespan is a minimum of 10 years under standard use. Individual damaged modules can be replaced without disassembling the entire surface.

Does the floor meet fire safety and sports standards?

ICE COVERTAN complies with PN-EN 14904 (sports surfaces), PN-EN 13501-1 (fire classification), DIN 18032-2 (sports halls), ISO 8302 (thermal resistance) and ASTM F2772 (multi-purpose surfaces). GAMAK Ice Cover has a B1 fire classification.

What is the difference between ICE COVERTAN and GAMAK Ice Cover?

ICE COVERTAN is a modular system with dual-layer modules connected by puzzle locks, insulation of 0.044 W/mK and weight of 6.2 kg/m². GAMAK Ice Cover is a panel system with a waffle/grid structure, 25.4 mm thickness and dimensions of 2,445 x 1,222.5 mm. Both systems allow covering an ice rink without defrosting the ice and hosting events, sports and concerts on it.

How many GAMAK Ice Cover panels are needed for a standard ice rink?

For a 26 x 60 m ice rink (1,560 m²), approximately 540 panels are needed. Each panel measures 2,445 x 1,222.5 mm (approx. 3 m²). The exact number depends on the ice sheet shape and required edge trimming.

Which system should I choose — ICE COVERTAN or GAMAK Ice Cover?

ICE COVERTAN is ideal where top insulation parameters, compliance with sports standards and fast modular installation are priorities. GAMAK Ice Cover is a good choice when you value a proven waffle/grid construction, durability and maintenance-free operation. Contact GAMAK — we will recommend the optimal system based on your rink's parameters and intended applications.
